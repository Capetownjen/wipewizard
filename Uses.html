Everywhere the magic works

From soap scum to scuff marks, here's where Wipe Wizard earns its name — and how to get the most out of every pad.

Effective uses

Built for hard surfaces around the house

🚿

Bathroom surfaces

Glass shower doors and ceramic tiles come up clear. Also great on soap scum and hard water stains.

👟

Sneakers & outdoor gear

Freshen up sneakers, patio furniture, and grill grates without a drop of chemical cleaner.

🖼️

Walls, baseboards & doors

Lift scuffs and crayon marks off walls, skirting boards and doors without damaging paintwork.

🔥

Kitchen appliances & stovetops

Cuts through grease and burnt-on food on stovetops and other kitchen appliances.

🧹

Floors

Tackle scuffed tile and grout lines — pair with the grout tip below for stubborn stains.

🍳

Kitchens

Countertops, splashbacks and hobs — anywhere everyday grease and grime builds up.

Pro tips

Get more out of every pad

✦

Grout gone gray?

For tough grout stains, sprinkle on baking soda, mist with water, and scrub with Wipe Wizard for like-new results.

✦

Make it last

Cut a pad into smaller pieces to extend its use across several smaller jobs instead of one whole pad at a time.

✦

Save the scraps for tough jobs

Wipe Wizard can wear down quickly on rough surfaces like shoe rubber. Keep the residual pieces from other jobs and use them near the end of their life for tasks like this.

Safety & composition

Good to know before you scrub

What it's made of

  • Wipe Wizard is made from melamine foam, a non-toxic plastic polymer.
  • It contains no added fragrance, bleach, or alcohol.
  • Not recommended for dishes — it can leave residue behind.

Precautions

  • Avoid high-gloss, polished or dark surfaces — stainless steel, copper, non-stick coatings and vehicle exteriors can dull or scratch.
  • Don't use on skin, as it can cause abrasions.
  • Keep away from toddlers and pets to prevent ingestion.
  • Never pair with bleach or alcohol-based products — this will damage the pad.
  • Wipe Wizard cleans through abrasion — always test a non-visible area first.
